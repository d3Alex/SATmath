Algebra. Sample task 1

Derek is the 11th tallest person in the room and the 9th shortest. How many people are in the room?

  • To solve this question let's draw the line where points are heights of people in the room:

    Derek is the 11th tallest person in the room. Therefore there are 10 people taller than Derek

    Derek is the 9th shortest person in the room. Therefore there are 8 people shorter than Derek

    The get the total number of people in the room, we need to add 8 (shorter than Derek), 1 (Derek) and 10 (taller than Derek)

    So, the total number of people in the room is 19.

Algebra. Sample task 2

The number of trees in a forest decreased by 25% from 2005 to 2006. If there were n trees in the forest in 2006, how many trees were there in 2005, in terms of n?

  • 6 ⁢ n 5
  • 5 ⁢ n 4
  • 4 ⁢ n 3
  • 3 ⁢ n 2

  • Let's x be the number of trees in 2005. The number of trees in 2006 is 75% of x, which can be expressed as 0.75x or 3x/4, thus n = 3x/4. Solving n = 3x/4 for x gives x = 4n/3 Therefore, the correct answer is C: 4n/3

Algebra. Sample task 3

Marisa needs to hire at least 10 staff members for an upcoming project. The staff members will be made up of junior directors, who will be paid $640 per week, and senior directors, who will be paid $880 per week. If Marisa's budget for staff salaries is $25,600 per month, how many junior directors and senior directors can she hire?

  • To find the number of junior directors and senior directors Marisa can hire, we need to:

    1: Convert the monthly budget to a weekly budget Monthly budget / 4 weeks per month = Weekly budget $25,600 / 4 = $6,400 weekly budget

    2: Allocate the weekly budget between junior directors and senior directors Let x be a number of junior directors and y be a number of senior directors Weekly budget = 640x + 880y

  • 3. describe the task conditions as system inequalities: { x + y ≥ 10 640 ⁢ x + 880 ⁢ y ≤ 6400

    Let's start with x = 10 and y = 0, which satisfy both conditions and is the upper limit of the second inequality. Any increase of y that keeps the first condition satisfied (x = 9 and y = 1 for example), will break the second condition. therefor x = 10 and y = 0 is the only solution and Marisa can hire 10 junior directors and 0 senior directors with her $25,600 per month budget

Algebra. Sample task 4

a ⁢ x 3 + b ⁢ x 2 + c ⁢ x + d = 0

In the equation above, a, b, c, and d are constants. If the equation has roots -1 , -3 , and 5, which of the following is a factor of a ⁢ x 3 + b ⁢ x 2 + c ⁢ x + d = 0 ?

  • x - 1
  • x + 1
  • x - 3
  • x + 5

  • The roots of the equation are given and are: -1, -3, and 5 To find a factor of the original equation, we can use the roots. The factors will be of the form (x - root), where the root is one of the given values.

    The possible factors are: (x + 1), since -1 is a root (x + 3), since -3 is a root (x - 5), since 5 is a root

    x + 1 is a the only factor at the answer choices and therefore the correct answer is: B) x + 1
