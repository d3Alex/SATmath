Problem Solving. Sample task 1

The same 20 contestants, on each of 3 days, answered 5 questions in order to win a prize. Each contestant received 1 point for each correct answer. The number of contestants receiving a given score on each day is shown in the table below.

5 out of 5 4 out of 5 3 out of 5 2 out of 5 1 out of 5 0 out of 5 Total
Day 1 2 3 4 6 2 3 20
Day 2 2 3 5 5 4 1 20
Day 3 3 3 4 5 3 2 20
Total 7 9 13 16 9 6 60

No contestant received the same score on two different days. If a contestant is selected at random, what is the probability that the selected contestant received a score of 5 on Day 2 or Day 3, given that the contestant received a score of 5 on one of the three days?

  • Probability is a ratio where a nominator is all succesful outcomes and denominator is all outcomes choosen.

    The total number of contestants receiving score 5 is 7. The total number of contestants receiving score 5 on Day 2 or Day 3 is 5, so the ansver is 5 ∕ 7

Problem Solving. Sample task 2

A square field measures 10 meters by 10 meters. Ten students each mark a randomly selected region of the field; each region is square and has side lengths of 1 meter and no two regions overlap. The students count the earthworms contained in the soil to a depth of 5 centimeters beneath the ground's surface in each region. The results are shown in the table below.

Region Number of Earthworms Region Number of Earthworms
A 107 F 141
B 147 G 150
C 146 H 154
D 135 I 176
E 149 J 166

Which of the following is a reasonable approximation of the number of earthworms to a depth of 5 centimeters beneath the ground's surface in the entire field?

  • 150
  • 1,500
  • 15,000
  • 150,000

  • The volume of square field is: 10 × 10 × 0.005 m 3 The volume of a region selected by each student is 1 × 1 × 0.005 m 3 ,
    therefor volume of square field is (10 × 10 × .005} ∕ (10 × 1 × 1 × .005) = 10 times bigger than region selected by all ten student.

    The total number earthworms in these ten regions is: 107 + 147 + 146 + 135 + 149 + 141 + 150 + 154 + 176 + 166 = 1471

    it would be a good approximation to multiply the total number earthworms counted by students by 10 to get
    the total number of earthworms in the entire field: 1471 × 10 = 14,710
    The closest to this number option is (C), thus (C) is the correct answer.
